Impact Breakdown
How it helps

Even with just three bins in our prototype, the same logic can scale to hundreds of bins in a city. The impact comes from three main areas:

  • Optimized routes: trucks drive shorter distances and avoid empty bins.
  • Food waste line: organic waste becomes compost and energy instead of methane in landfills.
  • Cleaner recycling: AI sorting and better bin guidance reduce contamination.
Less fuel burned Lower methane emissions Higher material recovery
City-Wide Scenario
Example Deployment

Imagine the SmartWaste system scaled to a district with 250 smart bins and a small fleet of collection trucks:

  • Up to 50–70 fewer truck trips per month in that district.
  • Several tonnes of food waste diverted to the Food → Energy line.
  • Cleaner recycling stream thanks to AI-supported sorting at HQ.

All numbers are illustrative, used for explaining the concept in this university project.

Link to Sustainability Goals
Framework

The SmartWaste concept supports multiple sustainability goals and policies:

  • Climate action: lower CO₂ and methane emissions from waste operations.
  • Responsible consumption & production: more materials recovered, less waste.
  • Sustainable cities: cleaner public spaces and more efficient public services.

Educational & Community Angle
People

Because our system is digital and visual, it can also be used as a live educational tool:

  • Schools can see live bin levels and understand how much waste is produced.
  • Public dashboards can motivate residents to sort waste correctly.
  • City planners can test “what-if” scenarios before investing in new routes or facilities.
