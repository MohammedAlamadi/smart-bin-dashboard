Food Waste → Clean Energy Line

The Food Waste Bin in our prototype is more than just a sensor. In a full city deployment, the food stream is sent to a Food Waste Line at HQ, where it is turned into compost and biogas instead of going to landfill.

Food Waste Processing Line
From smart bins to compost & energy
Process

Only food waste from the dedicated bin enters this line. This makes the stream cleaner, easier to treat, and safer to convert into useful products.

  • Trucks collect food waste separately from general waste.
  • At HQ, waste is shredded and mixed inside sealed digesters.
  • Microorganisms break down the organic matter without oxygen (anaerobic digestion).
  • The process releases biogas and leaves behind nutrient-rich digestate.
  • Digestate is cured into compost; biogas is refined into usable energy.

Compost Output
Closing the loop for soil health
Compost

The solid digestate from the digester is matured into compost. This can be used in:

  • Landscaping around the city (parks, roundabouts, gardens).
  • Urban farming projects and school gardens.
  • Replacing chemical fertilizers in some applications.
Biogas & Energy
Powering part of the city services
Energy

The biogas can be cleaned and used to:

  • Generate electricity for the waste facility itself.
  • Support EV charging for collection vehicles.
  • Provide heat or steam for industrial processes.
Why This Matters
Environmental & social impact
Benefits
  • Less food waste going to landfill → lower methane emissions.
  • Local compost production → greener parks & better soil.
  • Energy recovery → supports SmartWaste trucks & HQ.
  • Educational opportunity → schools can visit the facility.
Connection to Our Physical Prototype
From ESP32 bin on the table to a full Food Waste Line
Prototype Link

On the table we have only one Food Waste Bin, but it is already collecting the data needed for this line: fill level and smell (gas) readings are sent in real-time to Firebase. The values you see on the Live Dashboard are the same type of data that a real city deployment would use to:

  • Decide when to schedule a dedicated food-waste collection route.
  • Monitor smell spikes that may indicate high decomposition.
  • Estimate how much food waste is available for compost & energy production.
